The Thoughts about Ideal Career

To others, I am outgoing. However, I think I am an introverted creature. I prefer familiar people to unfamiliar people. I love new and unusual things. When I was a freshman, I believed I should apply for a job in an Export & Import company.

My BF made me understand that only do what I like can I stick to it. To some extent, it changed my course. Apprently, not a foreign trade personnel, translator or teacher. That’s why I choose a direction which is different from my major —— Business English.

In order to confirm whether it suits me. I tested on the Holland Code Quiz’s website. My result is as the follows:

  • Realistic:11

  • Investigative:14

  • Artistic:13

  • Social:13

  • Enterprising:10

  • Conventional:14

I have to say that is a complex result , the majority of it really tally with my personality. From different aspects, it provides with many positions, such as, auditor,conservator or programmer. Some of them are fascinating although I never heared of them before.

I did many part-time jobs in the past three years, clothing packer, waitress, salesperson, etc. However, to be honest, I didn’t like those jobs deeply. The were not my ideal positions. As for the future, I will look for the job of User Interface Design. My decison was by no means without foundation. On the one hand, it depends on my interest; on the other hand, I finished the first step of my future planning though it was a tiny step.

Nothing is so simple as it first seems, especially when you want to acquire new skills. What can I do to apply for dream position?

For me , I have to study all the subjects on my own. First, I have to learn basic books, such as ,Color Formation, Plane Formation and Three Dimentional. Second, I Have to learn how to use basic graphics softwares through Internet tutorial. Third, I formulated a studying schedule so as to improve my professional skills.

Maybe after 3 months even 3 years, I am still a novice. That’s not a question, I deeply believe that “Constant dripping wears away the stone“.
